# God Object — Vision

## Mission
A website for God Object — a software development company — that converts
visitors into paying clients and builds an audience for its own products.

## Target user
Two distinct audiences:

**Prospective clients** — businesses or individuals who need bespoke software
built. They are evaluating whether God Object has the technical depth and
credibility to be trusted with their project. They need to leave feeling that
God Object understands hard problems and solves them with craft.

**Emello early adopters** — technically curious home users who care about
privacy and are frustrated by Gmail/Outlook. They may not be software
professionals, but they are thoughtful about the tools they use. Later
audiences will include SMEs as Emello scales.

## Product vision
The God Object website leads with technical authority — the kind of place
that feels like it was built by people who think deeply about software. The
brand is bold and slightly unsettling: the logo (a towering black cube over
tiny human silhouettes) signals that the company understands the weight of
the moment in software history. Emello is showcased as flagship evidence of
that capability: a self-hosted, privacy-first, AI-native email client that
solves a real problem without compromise. The site has no filler — every
section earns its place by moving a visitor toward contact or signup.

## Key outcomes the product must deliver
- A prospective client who lands on the site understands what God Object does,
  believes in their technical ability, and takes the step of making contact.
- A privacy-conscious user who discovers Emello understands its value
  proposition immediately and joins the waitlist.
- The overall impression is competence, originality, and seriousness —
  not a generic agency template.

## What this is not
- Not a blog or content marketing platform.
- Not a client portal (no login, no project dashboards).
- Not a job board or careers page.
- Not a finished product showcase for Emello — it is an announcement/waitlist
  only; the product is not yet ready.
